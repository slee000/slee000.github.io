Selective Patterning of Conductive Elastomers Embedded with Silver Powders and Carbon Nanotubes for Stretchable Electronics


IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, 2022.

Figure 01. (a) Conceptual image of wearable device with magnified views of the strain gauge (red dotted box) and an example of a wearable sensor (blue dotted box). (b) Block diagram of the system setup for controlling the joint angle of the robotic hand based on the strain sensor signal. (c) A image of the robotic hand controlled by a hand. (d-e) Sensor outputs of (i) PIP joint and (ii) MCP joint of (d) the index finger and (e) the middle finger.

In this research, I used soft strain sensors to estimate finger joint angles and made a system where a robot hand imitates a human hand. We fabricated soft strain sensors using carbon nanotubes (CNTs) as resistors and silver powders as wires. The strain sensors that act as strain gauges could track joint angles of a finger, controlling the motions of a multi-fingered robotic hand. The signals of the strain gauges were obtained by a microcontroller unit (MCU) with an analog-to-digital converter (ADC) and a voltage follower. The MCU sent the sensor signals to a laptop through serial communication. The laptop converted the sensor signals to joint angles of the robotic hand and performed proportional-derivative (PD) closed-loop control of the joint angle of the robotic hand based on the estimated angles of the proximal interphalangeal (PIP) and metacarpophalangeal (MCP) joints of the index and middle fingers of the human hand with libraries (1).
